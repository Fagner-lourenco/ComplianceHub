POST

Description

This dataset gathers information about individuals' public exposure in open media sources, including associated journalistic content, aggregated visibility indicators, and the nature of the exposure, as well as sentiment analysis of the identified mentions. The returned news items are associated with the queried entity through inference based on the name identified in the content and, when provided, the keywords submitted in the query. Because the publications do not contain formal identifiers (such as CPF numbers), very common or short names may result in less precise associations or content unrelated to the queried individual.

For more information on business context, applications, and use cases, please refer to the Public Exposure documentation.

Technical name of the dataset: media_profile_and_exposure.

Limits and Pagination

This dataset allows you to limit the number of records returned using the modifier .limit(x) in the field Datasets And, when more records are available, it is possible to paginate the results using the modifier. .next(x).

To understand how to apply filters, limit the number of results, paginate, and customize the data returned, see the Customize Returns section.

Exposure indicators

The indicators returned summarize different dimensions of the entity's media presence:

MediaExposureLevel: indicates the level of exposure based on the total volume of news associated with the entity, considering, by default, the content identified in the last 6 months. The indicator reflects the intensity of the entity's media presence during the analyzed period.
CelebrityLevel: reflects the average monthly frequency with which the entity appears in journalistic content, calculated, by default, from mentions identified in the last 6 months. This indicator seeks to capture the recurrence of the entity's public visibility.
UnpopularityLevel: estimates the degree of unpopularity of the entity through a weighted average that considers the volume of negative news, the proportion of mentions with unfavorable sentiment, and the relevance of the sources in which the news was published. The calculation uses, by default, content from the last 6 months and distributes the result on a categorical scale of unpopularity.
Search by name

When the search is performed exclusively by name (key) name{XXXX} or name{XXXX}, shortname{XX}In cases where no other keys are sent or other datasets are included, the search is conducted without using an identity document (CPF). In these cases, the search independently considers:

the full name provided (name)
the shortname, when informed, or the first and last name derived from name

If the first and last name (shortnameEven if names are considered to have low uniqueness, the search is performed using only the full name, in order to avoid an excess of results for homonyms.

If other keys are sent in the query or other datasets are included in the request, full dataset processing will be applied.

Interpreting the field SentimentAnalysis

The field SentimentAnalysis It indicates the predominant sentiment identified in each news item associated with the consulted entity, based on text analysis models applied to journalistic content.

Label	Description
NEGATIVE	Predominance of negative sentiment
POSITIVE	Predominance of positive sentiment
NEUTRAL	Absence of a clear predominance of feeling.
UNDEFINED	It was not possible to infer sentiment.
POLARIZED	Simultaneous presence of positive and negative feelings.
SLIGHTLY_POSITIVE	Positive trend with neutrality
SLIGHTLY_NEGATIVE	Negative trend with neutrality
